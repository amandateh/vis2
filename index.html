Global Energy Consumption

Energy is a fundamental part of modern life, powering everything from homes to industries. To achieve a more sustainable future where everyone enjoys equal access to clean, reliable energy, it's crucial to first understand the challenges of our current consumption patterns. This visualization aims to offer insights into global energy use, helping to highlight the areas where change is most needed to ensure a balanced and sustainable energy future for all.

Primary Energy Consumption per Capita (2022) Across Selected Countries

This bar chart shows the Primary Energy Consumption per Capita in 2022 across selected countries. Qatar leads with the highest consumption, followed by Singapore and Canada. Malaysia, highlighted in green, ranks mid-range with about 40,000 kWh per person. Countries like Bangladesh, Pakistan, and Philippines have much lower energy use, highlighting the differences in energy consumption across regions.

These radial graphs display the distribution of primary energy consumption by source for different regions, comparing the World with other coutries/regions.

In the World view, the energy mix is dominated by Oil and Coal, followed by Hydro, Nuclear, and other renewable sources like Wind and Solar. This indicates the heavy dependence on fossil fuels globally, while renewable sources like Wind and Solar occupy smaller segments, showing their growing but still limited role in the global energy mix.

In contrast, Malaysia's energy consumption mix also relies significantly on Oil and Coal, but with a notable portion coming from Hydro energy, reflecting the country's investment in hydroelectric power. Smaller portions of energy come from Solar and Other renewables, signaling Malaysia's gradual shift towards cleaner energy sources.

These visualizations highlight the global reliance on traditional fossil fuels, while showing some movement towards renewable energy in certain regions, with Hydro standing out as a key renewable in Malaysia's energy landscape.


Advancing Toward Sustainable Energy Solutions

This Proportional Symbol Map highlights the varying adoption rates of low-carbon electricity across countries in 2022. Countries with larger circles, like those in Europe and parts of South America, show higher percentages of low-carbon electricity in their energy mix, while smaller circles represent lower adoption rates. Malaysia, with a share of 19.04%, lags behind many regions, signaling the need for greater investment in cleaner energy solutions to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and support a sustainable future.
